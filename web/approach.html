gitGost · Approach to comments

How we keep anonymous conversations useful.
← Back to home

gitGost allows anonymous contributions, but we work to keep conversations useful. This is a deliberate effort so anonymity doesn't degrade quality.

Prohibited content: no links, issues, or comments involving religion, violence, spiritism, sexual immorality, alcohol, drugs, weapons, pornography, or profanity. Detected content will be removed.

  • Don't post or upvote hollow links. We want material that teaches something real, not shallow curiosity.
  • “Truly interesting” means it leaves learning or context. Avoid gossip, flamebait, or empty politics.
  • Key principle: thoughtful comments. Civility + substance. Does your comment add new context or personal experience?
  • Empty negative comments (just insults) don't help; a brief “thanks” can.
  • Rule of thumb: don't write what you wouldn't say face to face. Debate is welcome; insults are not.

If you're right, your arguments stand without put-downs. Curiosity beats noise.
