gitGost · Karma

How the anonymous reputation tied to your hash works.
← Back to home

What is karma? It’s the anonymous reputation linked to your hash. It rises when your contributions are useful and drops when abuse is reported. It doesn’t expose your identity; it only signals trustworthiness.

How it works

  • Every anonymous hash starts with karma 0.
  • If your comments or issues are accepted or helpful, karma goes up.
  • If they’re reported for abuse or spam, karma goes down.
  • Karma travels with you while you use the same hash (or optional user token).

Why it matters

Good karma makes your anonymous contributions trustworthy to maintainers without revealing who you are. Low karma can block posts until reviewed.

Privacy first

We don’t store personal data: only the anonymous hash, its karma, and the relationship to the messages sent. We don’t track IPs or link identities.
